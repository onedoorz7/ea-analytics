The ICP Lead Scoring Framework

Score every lead on 6 dimensions. Prioritize what matters. Close more deals.
The problem: Most teams treat all leads equally. Your best reps waste hours on poor-fit leads while Prime ICPs wait in the queue. The fix: a data-driven scoring system that tells you exactly which leads deserve attention — built from your own CRM data.

The Challenge

A B2B SaaS company had thousands of leads in their CRM, but no systematic way to prioritize them. Sales reps were reaching out to everyone — burning time on low-fit prospects while high-value leads went cold in the queue.

They needed a scoring system that could automatically identify their best opportunities and route them to the right reps at the right time.

The Approach: 6 Scoring Dimensions

We built a lead scoring model based on six key dimensions. Each lead is scored on demographics (who they are) and behavior (how engaged they are):

🏢

Industry

Does the company operate in your target vertical?

📏

Company Size

Is this company in your sweet spot range?

👤

Job Title

Is this person a decision-maker or influencer?

🎯

Intent / Lead Source

How strong is their buying signal?

🌍

Region

Are they in a market you can serve well?

📡

Channel

How did they find you?

Key insight: The weights for each dimension come from your own win/loss data — not assumptions. Every business is different.

The 4-Tier System

Total score maps to a tier. Each tier triggers a different response:

🟢

Prime ICP

High-touch outreach. Best reps assigned.

Response: < 1 hour
🔵

Strong ICP

Priority follow-up. Personal outreach.

Response: same day
🟠

ACP

Standard nurture. Automated sequence.

Response: 24-48 hours
⚫

Out of Scope

Auto-nurture or deprioritize.

Low priority

The Results

Once the scoring system was implemented, the team could instantly identify which leads deserved immediate attention. Sales reps started focusing on Prime and Strong ICP leads, while lower-tier leads went into automated nurture sequences.

The impact was immediate: better prioritization, clearer marketing accountability, and a system that improved over time as more data came in.
