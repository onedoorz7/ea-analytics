Stop Counting Leads. Start Measuring Quality.

The framework for turning raw lead volume into a quality score your team can actually act on.
The problem: A campaign that brings 500 Out-of-Scope leads is worse than one that brings 50 Prime ICPs. But most teams can't tell the difference — they just count leads. Volume ≠ quality. And without a quality metric, you're flying blind on marketing ROI.

The Challenge

A B2B marketing team was being judged on "number of MQLs" — but they had no idea how many of those leads were actually good fits. Some campaigns generated hundreds of leads that never converted. Others brought in smaller numbers but closed at high rates.

They needed a way to measure lead quality, not just quantity — and prove which marketing channels were actually driving revenue.

The 4-Step Quality Framework

We built a system to turn their CRM data into a clear quality score:

Score

Apply ICP scoring to every lead

Remove noise to see true quality

Step 1: Score Every Lead

Apply your ICP scoring model across all 6 dimensions — Industry, Company Size, Job Title, Intent, Region, and Channel. Every lead gets a total score, plus bonus points for funnel engagement.

Step 2: Segment Into Tiers

Each scored lead falls into one of four tiers: Prime ICP, Strong ICP, ACP, or Out of Scope. This converts a raw number into language your team can act on.

Step 3: Filter Out the Noise

Not all leads in your CRM are real opportunities. Remove:

  • Duplicates — Multiple contacts from the same org
  • Unqualified — Students, job seekers, wrong company type
  • Partners — Channel partners tracked separately
  • Community users — Free-tier accounts not sales-qualified

This step often removes 50%+ of raw pipeline — and reveals the true quality picture.

Step 4: Track Quality Over Time

Monitor the tier distribution month over month. Is the share of Prime + Strong leads growing? This is your ongoing dashboard for marketing effectiveness.

Your North Star Metric

Once you've scored, segmented, and filtered — you need one number to track:

The Quality Metric
% Prime + Strong ICP
Of your filtered, qualified leads — what share are truly high-fit?

This single metric replaces "number of MQLs" as your marketing compass. It answers: are we attracting the right people, or just more people?

Key Takeaways

  • Volume is a vanity metric. 1,000 leads mean nothing if 800 are out of scope.
  • Filtering changes everything. Most CRMs are 50%+ noise. Clean data reveals true performance.
  • Track % Prime + Strong, not total lead count. This is your real marketing health metric.
  • Different channels have different quality profiles. Paid, organic, events, and partnerships all produce different ICP mixes.
