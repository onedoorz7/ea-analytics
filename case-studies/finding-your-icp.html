ICP Discovery

How to Find Your Ideal Customer Profile Using Your Own Data

Stop guessing. Your CRM already knows who your best customers are.
The problem: Most ICPs are based on gut feeling, copied from competitors, or defined once and never updated. Meanwhile, your CRM holds thousands of data points about who actually buys — and who doesn't. The answers are already there. You just haven't asked the right questions yet.

The Challenge

A B2B company had an ICP document — but it was based on assumptions from three years ago. Marketing was targeting one persona, while sales kept closing deals with a completely different type of customer.

They needed a data-driven process to discover who their actual best customers were, not who they thought they should be targeting.

The Approach: Data-Driven ICP Discovery

We started with their closed-won deals and worked backwards. The goal: analyze win rates and deal sizes across multiple dimensions and let the data reveal the truth.

The 6 Dimensions

We analyzed their deals across six key dimensions:

  • Industry — Which verticals close at the highest rate? Which have the largest deals?
  • Company Size — What's the sweet spot? Too small = low value, too large = long cycles?
  • Job Title / Seniority — Who are the actual buyers? Directors? Engineers? C-suite?
  • Region — Where do you win most? Geographic patterns often surprise.
  • Lead Source / Intent — Which sources produce customers, not just leads?
  • Channel / First Touch — Direct vs. paid vs. organic — what actually converts?

For each dimension, we calculated win rates and average deal sizes. The gaps between segments were dramatic — some performed 3-5x better than others.

From Data to ICP

Once the patterns emerged, we consolidated the top-performing segments into a tiered ICP definition:

Prime ICP Best-fit across most dimensions. Highest win rate, largest deals.
Strong ICP Good fit. Strong on key dimensions, may miss on one or two.
Acceptable Moderate fit. Worth pursuing but not prioritizing.
Out of Scope Poor fit based on data. Deprioritize or auto-nurture.

Key insight: This tiered ICP becomes the foundation for a lead scoring system you can implement directly in your CRM.

The Impact

With a data-driven ICP in hand, marketing could finally target the right personas in the right industries. Sales knew which leads to prioritize. And the company had a shared language — "This is a Prime lead" — that aligned both teams around the same definition of quality.

Most importantly, the ICP wasn't static. They reviewed it quarterly as new deals closed, refining the model based on actual outcomes.
