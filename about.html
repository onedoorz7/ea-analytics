Hi, I'm Elisha

Building with depth in the age of speed.

The Unconventional Path

I'm what you might call a "late bloomer" — and I've learned that's actually an advantage.

Before analytics, I worked in film production, coached football, and did youth work. I come from a creative household, and I've always been drawn to understanding how things work — systems, people, stories, data.

That background taught me something critical: the best insights come from connecting dots across domains, not just drilling deeper into one.

From Startup to IPO

I joined Riskified's New York office as a data analyst when it was still a growing startup. I worked closely with customer-facing teams and got to experience American business culture firsthand.

Back in Israel, I became a product analyst — the "data guy" for a squad (a startup within the company). I defined how data was saved, transformed, and used. I built dashboards, defined KPIs, and ran analyses that shaped product decisions.

Later, I became an internal "freelancer" — tackling strategic initiatives across the business. That role taught me I could deliver key insights in new domains, fast.

I stayed through the IPO, learning what it takes to scale data infrastructure and decision-making from 50 people to 500+.

"I realized I have what it takes to deliver key insights within new domains and relatively short timeframes — that's the generalist's advantage."

Why EA Analytics

After getting my MBA from Tel Aviv University (including a semester at Copenhagen Business School), I knew what I wanted to build: a consulting practice that brings enterprise-level analytics thinking to startups that can't afford a full-time team.

Most small-to-medium startups (under 50 people) don't have a dedicated analytics function. They're flying blind on:

  • Which leads are worth chasing (and which are burning money)
  • What their ideal customer profile actually looks like
  • Where deals are getting stuck in the sales funnel
  • Which marketing channels are working (and which are vanity metrics)

That's where I come in. I help you turn messy data into clear decisions — fast.

How I Work

I'm not here to sell you a six-month engagement and disappear. I take a proof-of-concept approach: we start with a focused analysis (often free for the right fit), you see the value, and we decide together what comes next.

My philosophy is simple:

  • Depth over speed. Fast answers are useless if they're wrong.
  • Authentic relationships over volume. I work with clients I believe in.
  • Concrete outcomes over buzzwords. No "data-driven transformation journeys" — just clear insights and action plans.
